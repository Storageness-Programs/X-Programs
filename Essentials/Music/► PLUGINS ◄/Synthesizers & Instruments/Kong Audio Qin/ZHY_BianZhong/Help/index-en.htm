ZengHouYi BianZhong Chime-bells 23/65

Help File

ZengHouYi BianZhong, also known as the Chime-bells of Marquis Yi of Zeng, is one of the most precious excavations in the modern times, bearing both archeological and musical

importances. Kong Audio have sampled and recreated it for composers and musicians around the world to add this unique sound from thousand years ago to your work. We hope you will enjoy it.

 

Start the host sequencer of choice and load an instance of QIN RV. Click the "Browser" button, and you will see the Instruments Menu (only installed instruments are shown) and the Effects Menu. Click once to select an instrument, then click the "LOAD" button to load it up into QIN RV. Now QIN RV is ready for use.

 





Click the instrument icon on the left side of the rack to display and switch between the user interfaces of different instruments, as shown in the screenshot below：

START

*Click the preset loading window to display the preset list menu. Select one of the presets to load it as shown in the pic on the right. Details are as follows:

 

 

We'll use patch ZHY23_Mid_Bells as an example.

 

 

"ZHY23" is the name of the instrument. "Mid_Bells" means the bells on the middle rack. By looking at this patch name combination, we can see it is a patch made of bells on the middle rack from the 23 bells BianZhong instrument.

 

Select the "LAYERS" tab to see that some subsets are loaded among the 12 subset slots. Each slot can load different subsuets and further adjusting them, e.g., adjusting the amplitude envelope parameters.

 

As you can see, slot 1 and 2 have subsets loaded up. The red color means the subset layer has Extender applied. Click "Edit" button you will see the default amplitude control interface, as shown in the pic below on the right.

 

 

Click the "Layout" button to enter the Subset Layer Layout editor. Here you can see sublayer 1/2 have loaded two subsets: "Mid_Centre_2" and "Mid_Centre1". The blue frames on the right shows the velocity ranges of these two subsets. "Mid_Centre1" will be triggered when the incoming MIDI velocity is between 0-63; Mid_Centre2 will be triggered when it is higher than 63.

Click "KEYMAPS" tab to swtich to the keymapping page. Here you can see all the velocity/key mappings of the subsets. *Note that in QIN engine, the basic unit for mapping is "subset". A subset is a pre-arranged set of samples.

 

  Click on the instrument icon on the left will bring up the main page for that instrument, as shown in the pic below:

 

 

The first loaded instrument is set to MIDI channel 2 by default. If more than one

instrument is loaded, QIN RV will assign MIDI channels automatically. The default audio

 output channel is always assigned to Out 1. The audio output channel can be manually

 changed if needed.

 

 

Now we will dissect ZHY65_SET1_Mellow.

 

 

"ZHY65" is the name of the instrument. "Set 1" means the full set of the 65 bells set. Mellow means the basic sound texture. By looking at this patch name combination, we can see it is a patch made of the full set of 65 bells and is more suitable for melancholic style of music.

 

Select the "LAYERS" tab to see that some subsets are loaded among the 12 subset slots. Each slot can load different subsuets and further adjusting them, e.g., adjusting the amplitude envelope parameters.

 

As you can see, slot 1-6 have subsets loaded up. The red color means the subset layer has Extender applied. Click "Edit" button you will see the default amplitude control interface, as shown in the pic below on the right.

 


Click "KEYMAPS" tab to swtich to the keymapping page. Here you can see all the velocity/key mappings of the subsets. The velocity mappings are 0-127 for the 6 subset slots loaded here, as shown in the pic below on the left. Click "KEYRANGE" button to bring up the key-mapping range where you can see the mapping of the 6 subset slots. You can adjust them as needed. *Note that in QIN engine, the basic unit for mapping is "subset". A subset is a pre-arranged set of samples.

In BianZhong Pro(65 pcs), the SET1-3 in the preset names refer to these three different sets of the whole complete set.
